## What’s Happening

We held two major events for the semester: BENTO 🍱, a take on a spring demo-day like event (in parallel with our fall demo days, recently [spill ≋](https://spill.purduehackers.com)) and we hosted Sentry at Purdue for a fall recruiting event!

[BENTO 🍱](https://bento.purduehackers.com/) was a presentation night where over a dozen speakers from the Purdue Hackers community got 15 minutes to present on a topic of their choice. We had about a hundred audience members and the event lasted for four hours, covering topics such as Predictive Coding Models, Training Drones, Building DJ Decks, Porting Windows and PS3 Games to macOS, and many many more topics. You can watch the recording of the event [on our YouTube Channel](https://youtube.com/watch?v=).

We hosted [Sentry](https://sentry.io/welcome) at Purdue! They flew out founder David Cramer, a recruiter, and two engineers to come meet students! We helped them give a fun and engaging tech talk and they kindly sponsored Hack Night where they hung out with Purdue Hackers members until midnight! It was a lot of fun and we enjoyed having them on campus, and they enjoyed it so much they've decided to join the CS Career Fair and host more events with us in the fall! Exciting times!

As we wrap up the semester, we're seeing an influx of new members thanks to our events and [extensive](https://www.purdueexponent.org/campus/events/purdue-hackers-hosts-bento/article_3a5fe46f-296c-4fff-9d52-3370118df308.html) [press](https://www.purdueexponent.org/campus/events/purdue-hackers-bento-brings-lightning-talks-and-creative-project-to-campus/article_aa742f4a-9a14-4272-940c-f956e046076f.html) [coverage](https://www.purdueexponent.org/campus/events/hack-night-turns-side-projects-into-friendships/article_6c095eb5-54cf-43ef-b2b3-e45b7428d637.html) of Purdue Hackers. The organizing team is excited to start thinking about what our programs look like this coming fall as we gear up for the incoming Class of 2030  -- who have already been joining the Purdue Hackers Discord server and expressing great interest in our organization!

## What We're Learning

We're currently spending our money on two fronts: µgrants and makerspace equipment. I recently purchased soldering equipment (out of pocket) for ~$100 to test whether students would benefit from being able to solder components at Hack Night. They've already seen use over the last eight weeks since we purchased them, and we've gotten more advanced equipment since (e.g. a multimeter) to better support students building hardware projects at Hack Night! We've made a new finance hire to help us find deals with equipment providers and we're continuing to building up our makerspace capabilities so we can provide more utilities to members at Hack Night. As one may expect, building a makerspace is expensive so as we build a budget for next fall, expect to see a new budget line added for makerspace equipment!

We're also moving towards a more "public-facing" build-in-public culture. We've made great progress since in getting members to share their work within the community, and now we're pushing to share their work with the world! To this end, our [Twitter account](https://x.com/purduehackers) is back in use and will actively retweet what folks are making and we've also created [ships.purduehackers.com](https://ships.purduehackers.com) so you can see what people are working on!
